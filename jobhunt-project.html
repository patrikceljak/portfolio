JOBHUNT

A Job Finding Mobile App for Students & Graduates

View Flow Prototype

  • Role

    UX/UI designer designing an app for job finding, from bottom to top.

  • Responsibilities

    Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs.

  • Duration

    December 2021 - January 2022

  • Problems

    Students and graduates have a hard time finding jobs related to their profession.

  • Goals

    Create an easy to use app, where they can share everything and through detailed search, find what they're looking for.

User research

  • I performed user research and went through empathy maps to understand the users I’m designing for. Through my research primary groups were people that are having a hard time to find jobs matching their profession description. This was an assumption I made going through, but with later research that wasn’t the only thing people were neglected with. Profession related problems were of the essence but so was having options to share their CVs/Resumes, chatting with companies and having an app that does searches for them.

    • User pain points

    Time

    Student full of assignments have a less time searching for job listings.

    Accessibility #1

    Other job finding sites lack the options for automatic searching based on their favorites.

    Accessibility #2

    Job listing sites don't have integrated cvs and resumes that automatically send when applying to a job.

    IA

    Cluttered UI gives users difficulty when trying to find jobs.

    Personas

    Problem statement:

    Amanda is a hard working student who needs to find a job because she wants to have earnings and experience.

    Paper Wireframes

    This time I went with a drawing app on desktop instead of drawing on paper. Going with it was much cleaner and I had more ideas coming through.

    Digital Wireframes

    After finishing my drawn wireframes, it was time to shift them to digital. Going through some design changes along the way.

    Low-Fidelity Prototype Flow

    Going through with studies I wanted more options for users. For example an option for going to a dedicated company page so they can follow them and see all their listings and many more features.

    View Low-fidelity Prototype

    Usability Study: Findings

    I conducted two usability studies that helped me make a better user experience and flow in the app. The first one served as to better the wireframes and low-fidelity prototypes and the second to finish the high-fidelity prototype and to see what needs refining.

    • Finding 1

      Difficulty navigating the search engine.

    • Finding 2

      Couldn't see job listings when visiting companies sites.

    • Finding 3

      Not understanding some iconography.

    Mockups

    Going through with the usability study I noticed that the screen was too cluttered so I changed the design a little and based on people having a hard time finding companies job listing I made a button for easy access.

    Final results of everything procesed together!

    Accessibility Considerations

    1. Accessibility for text translation and changing language of the app.

    2. Option for screen reading and press sensitivity.

    3. Accessibility for impaired vision changing of color schemes.

    My Takeaways

    • Impact:

      The app helps bring users and their needs together. As well as finding perfect jobs tailored by their professions.

    • What I learned:

      Designing the second UI/UX project helped me understand how users need to be in the center of the app, as well as understanding that the first designs aren’t the last. User feedback is really important and it helps the app to grow how it needs to be.
